Nintendo 64

Released in North America on September 26, 1996, the Nintendo 64 brought many classic games such as “The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time”, “Goldeneye007, and “Super Mario 64”. Aided by 64-bit CPU, the console was one of the first to introduce a generation of players to 3D graphics!

"Play has no limits."
Playstation 1

Predating the Nintendo 64 by a year, the PlayStation 1 was released on September 5,1995 in North America with well-known games such as “Final Fantasy 7”,” Metal Gear Solid”, and “Crash Bandicoot”. The PlayStation 1 was originally meant to be a console project that Nintendo and Sony were to make together, but due to complications, Sony went ahead and made the PlayStation on their own.

"Major fun and games!"
Sega Genesis

Released on August 14, 1989, The Sega Genesis, also known as the Mega Drive outside North America, is a 16-bit fourth generation home video game console developed and sold by Sega. It was Sega's third console and the successor to the Master System.
